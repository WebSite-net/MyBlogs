Blog Post 1: The Global Journey of My Pillow

Sharing my thoughts about pillows

How my pillow connects to world

Today, I’m going to talk about something we all have, but don’t usually think about: my pillow. It’s soft, comfy, and essential for a good night’s sleep. What does it have to do with globalization? Hello, my name is Nairouz Sido and welcome to my first blog post! Today we will explore how my pillow became the pillow that I know of. Before I go into the specifics, what is globalization? According to my lectures on globalization, different academic disciplines have different definitions of what globalization is. Although it lacks a clear "definition," many scholars can agree that it shares certain similar elements, including interconnectivity, the strengthening of international ties, and the cross-border movement of people, goods, and ideas. Globalization is made possible in large part by technological developments in information sharing, communication, and transportation. (Stockdale, 2024).

So what's the connection between globalization and a pillow? The materials used to make my pillow came from different areas of the world: the synthetic polyester stuffing was sourced from a factory in China, the cotton cover was made in India, and the pillow itself was built in Vietnam. It finally made it to the store where I bought it here in Canada after traveling across oceans (Campbell, 2024). The journey is a reflection of the vast worldwide supply systems that our class has been learning. My pillow would not be around today if international trade hadn't been coordinated. Many nations were engaged in the production of the finished product, from raw materials to it, demonstrating the interdependence that globalization symbolizes. Crossborder trade in goods, services, and technology connects economies and communities in ways that are frequently invisible. However, there are environmental consequences associated with international shipping, such as resource usage and carbon emissions. Positively, globalization makes it possible for citizens of other countries to engage in the global economy, creating jobs in the manufacturing, transportation, and retail industries. Despite its apparent simplicity, my pillow stands for the worldwide connection that characterizes contemporary life. The next time I go to sleep, I'll consider the long journey my pillow traveled, which will serve as a reminder of the complex ties that underlie everyday items.
